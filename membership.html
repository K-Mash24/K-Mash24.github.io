Types Of IEEE Membership..



IEEE Student Members

-The IEEE Students platform is a dedicated space for empowering the next generation of innovators and leaders in technology and engineering.

- As part of the globally recognized Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), it serves as a hub for student members to access resources, opportunities, and networks that foster professional growth and technical excellence.



IEEE Young Professionals (Graduate Members)

- IEEE Young Professionals is an international community of enthusiastic, dynamic, and innovative members and volunteers.

- Affiliation with IEEE Young Professionals is open to Graduate Students and higher-grade members with early career interests at no additional cost.

- IEEE Young Professionals provides career advantage resources and helps members leverage the power of the global network through its expanding Affinity Groups and volunteer opportunities.



IEEE Full (Professional) Members

- IEEE members are a community of more than 460,000 technology and engineering professionals united by a common desire to continuously learn, interact, collaborate, and innovate.

- IEEE Life Members are technology influencers, pioneers, and valuable partners sharing over one million years of experience with the next generation of innovators.

- IEEE “Life” status is automatically bestowed based on age and years of membership. IEEE Life membership (LM) is proof of a strong sustained commitment to the profession.


IEEE Societies Membership

- IEEE Society members stay technically current, network with colleagues locally and abroad, and collaborate on research and projects with leading experts, all while taking advantage of specialized opportunities.

- Discover which Society, or Societies, best match your technical interests.

- Take this opportunity to check them out and find which society aligns with your technical expertise/ preference.

  • How Can You Join Us?


    • Here are the steps to join IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers):


    1. Visit the IEEE website: The first step to joining IEEE is to visit the IEEE website at www.ieee.org. You will be able to find information about IEEE and the various membership options available.

    2. Choose your membership type: IEEE offers several types of memberships, including student, professional, and associate memberships. Choose the membership type that best fits your needs.

    3. Create an account: Once you have selected your membership type, create an account on the IEEE website. You will need to provide some basic information such as your name, address, email, and affiliation.

    4. Select your subscription: After creating an account, you will need to select a subscription plan. IEEE offers different membership plans, and you can choose the one that fits your needs.

    5. Complete your profile: Once you have selected your subscription plan, complete your profile by providing your educational background, work experience, and research interests.

    6. Pay your membership fee: Finally, pay your membership fee to complete the registration process. Once your payment is processed, you will receive a confirmation email with your IEEE membership number.
      - You can pay online using a credit card, Debit card/M-pesa Globalpay or PayPal. (Whichever is preferred)

      - If everything is done correctly, then you should pay a student member subscription fees of $14 for a full year. If you register between the 1st of March and the 15th of August you can pay $7 for half a year.

      - Note that subscribing into other societies may incur additional costs.

    7. Enjoy your membership: Once you have completed the registration process, you can start enjoying the benefits of your IEEE membership. You will have access to a wide range of resources, networking opportunities, and professional development programs.

      - You should have been provided with an IEEE licence/registation number if its your first time registering, its very important in order to enjoy all the IEEE benefits,


  • Member Resources.


    • IEEE XPLORE Digital Library



    - The IEEE Xplore digital library is a powerful resource for discovery of and access to scientific and technical content published by the IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) and its publishing partners.



    The content in IEEE Xplore comprises:


    • 195+ journals.

    • 1,800+ conference proceedings.

    • 6,200+ technical standards.

    • Approximately 2,000 eBooks.

    • 400+ online courses.


    IEEE Spectrum (Technology Magazine)



    - IEEE Spectrum is an award-winning technology magazine and the flagship publication of the IEEE, the world’s largest professional organization devoted to engineering and the applied sciences.

    - Our charter is to keep the public and over 400,000 IEEE Members informed about major trends and developments in technology, engineering, and science.

    - Our news stories, features, special reports, podcasts, videos, and infographics engage our readers with clear explanations about emerging concepts and developments with details they can’t get elsewhere.


    IEEE Collabratec



    - IEEE Collabratec is an Integrated online community for networking & collabration. Here, technology professionals can network, collaborate and create in one central hub.

    - IEEE Collabratec’s suite of productivity tools is available to technology professionals around the world, with exclusive features for IEEE members.


    IEEE Standards Association



    - The IEEE Standards Association (IEEE SA) is a globally recognized standards-setting body within IEEE that develops consensus standards through an open process that engages industry and brings together a broad stakeholder community.

    - IEEE standards are developed in a broad range of industries, including power and energy, artificial intelligence systems, internet of things, consumer technology and consumer electronics, biomedical and health care, learning technology, information technology and robotics, telecommunication, automotive, transportation, home automation, nanotechnology, information assurance, emerging technologies, and many more.

    - Discover new and emerging technologies and concepts, or bring your ideas for further technology exploration. Industry Connections (IC) Programs & Activities offer a safe-harbor accelerator, where industry professionals collaboratively explore diverse technologies to determine appropriate paths for use and development.
